Spokane

Situated along the picturesque Spokane River in eastern Washington, Spokane emerges as a vibrant and culturally rich city, proudly holding the title of the most populous in Spokane County. Nestled amidst the Selkirk Mountains, Spokane boasts a unique geographic location, inviting residents and visitors alike to explore its scenic surroundings. Serving as both the economic and cultural nucleus of the Spokane metropolitan area, the city embraces its identity as the birthplace of Father's Day and carries the local moniker "Lilac City."

Distinguished by its official nickname, Hooptown USA, Spokane comes alive annually with the hosting of Spokane Hoopfest, the world's largest basketball tournament. The city's dynamic spirit is further accentuated by landmarks like the Spokane International Airport, strategically positioned near Downtown Spokane. With a population that reflects growth and diversity, Spokane stands as the second-most populous city in Washington, fostering a thriving community that reached an estimated 597,919 in the broader Spokane Metropolitan Area in 2022.
