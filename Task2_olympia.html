Olympia

Named for its picturesque view of the Olympic Mountains, Olympia earned the distinction of becoming the capital of Washington even before achieving statehood in 1853. Initially incorporated as a town in 1859, it later attained city status in 1882. The region holds historical significance as it was cherished by numerous Native American tribes.

As Washington gained statehood in 1889, Olympia, already a burgeoning city, embarked on a journey of development. The city focused on enhancing its infrastructure, introducing amenities like hotels, street lighting, and advanced city water systems. Over time, these improvements contributed to Olympia's growth and development.

Today, Olympia stands as a vibrant city, home to a thriving community of nearly 56,000 residents. Its journey from a capital-in-waiting to a bustling urban center reflects not only its rich history but also its commitment to progress and providing a high quality of life for its residents.
